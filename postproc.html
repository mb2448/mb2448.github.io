Exoplanet image processing algorithms

A key aspect of exoplanet imaging is the use of post-processing to discover planets in noisy data, and significant improvements in sensitivity over the past decade have been realized by use of more powerful data analysis techniques. Post-processing is essential for ground-based data, and envisioned as a requirement for space-based observatories such as WFIRST, HabEx, and LUVOIR.

Modern post-processing methods almost all rely on feature subtraction, using dimensionality reduction techniques like principal components analysis or sparse decomposition to remove scattered starlight in the focal plane and reveal any planets underneath. These methods all suffer from a number of issues, including oversubtraction of the planet signal, many tunable parameters, and challenges in establishing robust confidence limits on the validity of a detection. Independent experts can disagree on the validity of a “detection” in a dataset even when using the same post-processing algorithm.

We are working on new postprocessing algorithms using techniques from Bayesian linear modeling. The goals of this work are improved detection of planets, robust measurements of uncertainties, and the ability to do model selection to quantify probabilities of detection in ambiguous data.
