Wavefront sensing and control

Wavefront sensing and control is the most important aspect of high contrast imaging, with the optical system needing to be accurate to nanometer levels to have a chance of detecting planets. Our group is involved in multiple projects in wavefront sensing and control, broadly in the areas of focal plane wavefront control and predictive control of adaptive optics..

Focal plane wavefront control

In focal plane wavefront control, quasi-static optical errors are sensed and corrected in the focal plane, meaning fully common-path correction. Instead of using separate wavefront sensing hardware (necessarily non common path), the deformable mirror shape is changed to determine the phase and amplitude of the electric field in the focal plane. Our current efforts are in upgrading the Keck telescope's adaptive optics capabilities to enable these capabilities. Preliminary results show factors of 5-10 increase in sensitivity, and we are working to make such gains a routine part of the planet imaging capabilities of the observatory.

Predictive control

Predictive control is focused on improving the performance of the adaptive optics system, which corrects atmospheric turbulence in the telescope at thousands of times per second. At some point, running faster ceases to give an improvement, as starlight measured per cycle drops to unusable levels. The solution is to use previous information to predict what the atmosphere will do in the future. Predictive control holds large promise for improving planet imaging on the ground.
