I am an experimental astrophysicist working on exoplanet detection. I pursue this goal by building and using ground and space-based instruments and developing new data analysis techniques.
I received BAs in physics and mathematics from Columbia University in 2008, and worked at a hedge fund as a professional stock trader for the following year before returning to scientific research in Amber Miller's CMB group. In 2010 I began graduate studies in astrophysics at the California Institute of Technology, jointly advised by Shri Kulkarni and John Johnson. During graduate school, I was supported by a NASA Space Technology Research Fellowship.
I received my PhD in 2016 and joined the staff of the Jet Propulsion Lab immediately afterwards.

Aside from astrophysics, I am interested in optics, electronics, and programming.
